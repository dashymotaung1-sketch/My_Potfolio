Java Chess Engine

"Developed a high-performance chess engine from scratch using Java. This project involved implementing the Minimax algorithm with Alpha-Beta pruning to optimize move searching. I focused heavily on memory management and bitboard representation to ensure the engine could evaluate thousands of positions per second. It demonstrates my ability to translate complex game theory into efficient, object-oriented code."

The Challenge: How do you search through millions of possibilities without the computer freezing or running out of memory?

The Challenge: Ensuring the engine doesn't crash or make an illegal move during these rare moments.

Fix: This is where your dedication and detail-orientation come in. You have to write "Unit Tests" for every possible scenario to ensure the engine follows the rules 100% of the time.

Fix: Implementing Alpha-Beta Pruning. This "prunes" branches of the logic tree that are clearly bad, allowing the engine to look deeper into the "good" moves without wasting CPU cycles.
